Projectile Motion

Projectile motion is the 2D movement of an object (a projectile) launched into the air, where the only force acting on it after launch is gravity (ignoring air resistance). The path followed is a parabola.

Key principle: The motion is broken down into two independent parts: Horizontal (x axis): Constant velocity because acceleration is zero (ax = 0) Vertical (y axis): Uniform acceleration due to gravity (ay = -g)

Time (t): This is the crucial link between the two independent motions

Apex (max height): At the highest point, the vertical velocity (Vy)) is momentarily zero
